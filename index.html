Research

This page collects my current paper and earlier thesis work. Click any item to expand the abstract.

  1. Stochastic Simulation of Chemotaxis

    2024

    Jingwu Hu. Bachelor Degree Final Year Project.

    Click to view abstract

    We reviewed the well-posedness of classical mean field McKean-Vlasov equations and propagation of chaos. We then introduced a chemotaxis variation in which particle motion is influenced by the distribution of chemicals rather than directly by other particles. The thesis provided a rigorous proof of existence and uniqueness for mean field solutions of the McKean-Vlasov chemotaxis dynamics and included simulations illustrating segregation behavior.

  2. On the Spatio-Temporal Increments of Nonlinear Parabolic SPDEs and the Open KPZ Equation

    Aug 2025

    Jingwu Hu, Cheuk Yin Lee. Submitted, arXiv:2508.05032.

    Click to view abstract

    We study spatio-temporal increments of solutions to nonlinear parabolic SPDEs on a bounded interval with Dirichlet, Neumann, or Robin boundary conditions. We identify exact local and uniform spatio-temporal moduli of continuity, establish small-ball probability estimates and Chung-type laws of the iterated logarithm, and extend some results to the open KPZ equation on the unit interval with inhomogeneous Neumann boundary conditions.
